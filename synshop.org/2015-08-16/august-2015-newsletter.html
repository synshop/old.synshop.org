August 2015 Newsletter

By Richard Furniss

What’s new this month?

Full Spectrum donated a desktop 3D stereolithography technology printer, a big thank you go’s out the Full Spectrum who is a big supporter of the Las Vegas hackerspace Synshop.

DEFCON was this month and once again the Synshop had a table in the learning Village where some 700 electronics con badges were assembled and other members participated in contests. We look forward to some of the attendees writing articles about their experiences at the conference.

We had a CubeX donated to the shop so if anyone is familiar with it or wants to learn about this piece of equipment please take the challenge and set it up and get it going.

The Shapeoko is getting close to cutting and drilling circuit boards (thanks to Pete) and Bill Livolsi has created an instructional user manual for the machine (thanks Bill) The search for a bigger space is still moving forward, stayed tune for further announcements.


What was built?

Shop member Bob Brandys finished his Star Trek engine room warp core replica and it is on display at the shop for the next 3 weeks


One of the big projects this month was a garden bed planter made from recycles pallets by Rachel Lewison who works for the state as a recycling coordinator


Other spaces

We had a visitors from the Vermont Hackerspace Laboratory B (http://www.laboratoryb.org/) they were attending DEFCON and wanted to stop by and see our space while there were seeing the downtown area. This is a small town home grown Hackerspace, they started in 2010 with 10 members and has grown to 35 members and are partnering with other organization to grow there membership.

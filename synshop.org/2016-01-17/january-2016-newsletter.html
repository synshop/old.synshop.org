January 2016 Newsletter

By Richard Furniss

What’s new this month

We will be having elections for the board members on January 30 at 2pm so please come to the board meeting and cast you vote for the people that will guide the shop through this year.

The exhaust vent for the welding area and the laser cutter have been ordered and should be installed in the next month or two.

The mini maker fair is coming up the end of February so if you want to get a booth to sell or want to show something that you made contact Bill Tomiyasu.

Work is progressing on the shop electrical system to power the equipment so look for construction and some minor disruptions in the work area.

What was built this month

Beer tap handle was laser engraved on the laser cutter and installed at Nacho Daddy's downtown

Rulers, design and cut your own ruler or caliper with the laser cutter. If you want to keep people from walking away with your Ruler or other office items you can customize them with your own logo or name. Or you can make a your own custom ruler or make your own “go” – “no go” gauges

Christmas decorations, Cut your own ornaments for the holidays and watch for a special cut day for Valentines day

Silhouette photos were made by using the laser cutter with a white paper as background and cut pieces of black glued to the background.

Upcoming Class’s

Every Thursday we will be discussing and learning CAD/CAM software. We are starting with the very beginning of Fusion 360 and Inkscape.

Don’t miss the Danger room tool orientation held every month

Other spaces

10bitWorks Hackerspace in San Antonio Texas, very nice hackerspace with a lot going on like Ham Radio, jewelry making, CNC metal mill check out their website

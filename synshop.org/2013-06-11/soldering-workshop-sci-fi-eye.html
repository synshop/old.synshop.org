Soldering Workshop with the Sci-Fi Eye


 

For this next Soldering Workshop, SYN Shop's very own Justin Kagan will be conducting a class to build up his Gizmaniac GM-01 Sci-Fi Eye.  When fully assembled, this device will simulate a robotic scanning ?eye?, as seen in numerous science fiction television shows and movies.  NOTE:  Pumpkin not included. :-)


All components are through-hole, making this kit a great place to start when you're learning to solder.


The cost of the class is $30, and includes the Gizmaniac Sci-Fi Eye kit.  Batteries will be provided with the kit so you can power up the device when you're done soldering and show off your handiwork.


The kit comes with a pre-programmed Atmel ATtiny85 microcontroller chip (the baby brother of the ATmega328P used on the Arduino Uno). The assembled device is VERY hackable, and includes a standard Atmel 6-pin in-circuit serial programming header for flashing your own custom firmware using the Arduino development environment after a few minor modifications.  If you are already familiar with or interested in Arduino or microcontrollers in general, this class will teach you to build a device that will start you down the path to another level of microcontroller skills.


Firmware re-flashing is not covered as part of this class, but if there is sufficient interest it could be considered for a future class.


PLEASE NOTE: There is a hard limit of 15 students for this class due to the number of available soldering stations at the shop.  If you have your own soldering station, please bring it and send us a note so we can allow for additional students who may wish to participate.  A generic run-of-the-mill soldering iron is not recommended, as it gives vastly inferior results than a temperature-controlled soldering station.  If the class is full and you are willing to bring your own equipment, additional kits may be available for purchase the day of the event on a first-come first-served basis.


For anyone opting to purchase their own soldering station (after all, you're learning to solder so you can build more things), you may wish to consider the following as decent, affordable options:
